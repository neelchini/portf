About Me

I'm a third-year Computer Science & Engineering student navigating the exciting chaos of coding. My typical workflow goes like this: I code, I debug, I cry a little, and then I code again; essentially functioning as a human compiler trying to transform curiosity into functional code (typically with only 71 errors per run, but who's counting?). I'm genuinely passionate about creating solutions while embracing the irony of pretending I know exactly what I'm doing.

I thrive on challenging projects that push my boundaries and introduce me to new technologies. Whether it's web development, network security, or exploring emerging fields, I'm always eager to dive deep into whatever captures my interest and expand my technical horizons.

During my first two semesters, I dedicated substantial time to competitive programming, solving problems across platforms like AtCoder, LeetCode, and Codeforces. That dopamine rush when a complex solution finally clicks? Absolutely everything.

Beyond the digital realm, I have a deep appreciation for pure sciences, with chemistry holding a special fascination for me. I also enjoy writing scientific content, breaking down complex concepts into engaging, accessible explanations that spark curiosity in others.
